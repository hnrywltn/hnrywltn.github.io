Operations

Working with other vehicle and software operators alongside extremely talented AI engineers gave me insight and inspiration for being a better programmer.

Troubleshooting

After driving a taxi in San Francisco for a while, self driving vehicles were my main gateway into the programming industry. There are a lot of moving parts that I eventually started to see meld together. I got to see different teams with different areas of expertise shine in their own unique ways. I was lucky to be on the operations side of things where I got to ride in, test, and troubleshoot robots.

At both Zoox(Amazon) and Cruise(General Motors) my main role was to ride in their robots through San Francisco and collect data. The other two crucial responsibilities were ensuring safety and troubleshooting.

This is where I found my love for not only how to solve problems but how to find them. With a strong propensity for seeing and remembering patterns, I excelled and still find it rewarding.

Team Work

Another extremely rewarding part of working with autonomous vehicles were the people.

I tend to think that people learn in different ways and the right approach in education can make or break a learning experience. In both companies I had the pleasure of starting as a trainee and moving up to education roles. Explaining how things work and break, ensuring safety, and collecting lots of good data is important...but! connection is the best tool for helping people understand the importance of why things are done in certain ways. I know team work is essential for connection and product integrity.
